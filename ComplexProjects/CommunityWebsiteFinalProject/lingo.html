The Language of AvGeeks

Ever heard an aviation fan get excited about a "livery" or a "heavy" and felt a little lost? Don't worry! Every community has its own language, and ours is just shorthand for the things we love. This page is your simple cheat sheet. We've gathered the most common terms you'll hear so you can follow along and start "speaking AvGeek" yourself.

Aviation Term Definition & Explaination Image Example
AvGeek (Pronounced "Av-Geek") Slang for an "Aviation Geek," or anyone who is extremely passionate about airplanes and flight.
Livery A plane's unique paint job, logo, and airline branding. Enthusiasts love seeing rare or "special" liveries.
Spotting The hobby of watching, photographing, and keeping a log of different aircraft, often done at an airport fence.
Tail Number The plane's unique "license plate" (e.g., N123AB). AvGeeks use this to track a specific plane's history.
ATC Short for Air Traffic Control. These are the people on the ground who guide pilots safely through the sky.
Wide-body A large passenger jet with two aisles, like a Boeing 747 or Airbus A380.
Narrow-body A smaller, more common passenger jet with a single aisle, like a Boeing 737 or Airbus A320 Family.
Heavy A term you'll hear pilots and ATC use. It refers to a large, heavy aircraft that creates a lot of wake turbulence. An aircraft is designated as "heavy" for its callsign if it has a maximum takeoff weight (MTOW) of 300,000 pounds (136,000 kg) or more.
Super A term you'll hear pilots and ATC use for an exceptionally large aircraft that creates the most significant wake turbulence. An aircraft is designated as "super" for its callsign if it is an Airbus A380 (or was the Antonov An-225). These aircraft have a maximum takeoff weight far exceeding the "heavy" threshold, typically over 1,200,000 pounds (544,000 kg).
Warbird A restored, classic military aircraft, most famously from World War II (like a Spitfire or P-51 Mustang).
Winglets The small, turned-up tips on the end of a plane's wings. They aren't just for looks, as they help save fuel as well!
